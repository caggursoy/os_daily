Subject: Recent Developments in Open Science Policies and Infrastructure

- Several national funding agencies have updated open access mandates, reinforcing public access to research.
- European institutions are significantly investing in open research infrastructures to enhance data sharing and sustainability.
- A community-driven peer review initiative is gaining traction, promoting transparency and collaboration in academic publishing.

### Policy
- **New Open Access Mandates Accelerate Global Open Science Adoption**  
  Several national research funding agencies announced updated open access requirements, aligning with Plan S principles for publicly funded research.  
  [Source](https://www.science.org/content/article/open-access-publishing-new-mandates-2026)

- **Open Licenses Reaffirmed as Default for Publicly Funded Research Outputs**  
  Open Science policymakers emphasized the necessity of permissive licenses like Creative Commons for enabling reuse of research outputs.  
  [Source](https://creativecommons.org/2026/01/open-licenses-research-outputs)

### Tools
- **Persistent Identifiers Expanded to Support Open Research Workflows**  
  Enhancements to persistent identifier systems will improve tracking of datasets and contributors in open research environments.  
  [Source](https://blog.datacite.org/new-pids-open-research-workflows)

### Research
- **Open Research Infrastructure Sees Rapid Investment Across Europe**  
  European universities are investing in open repositories and platforms to ensure compliance with FAIR data principles and sustainable practices.  
  [Source](https://www.nature.com/articles/open-research-infrastructure-update)

- **Community-Led Peer Review Gains Momentum in Open Science Communities**  
  An initiative launched by open science advocates aims to create a community-driven peer review model that complements traditional journals.  
  [Source](https://openscience.eu/news/community-peer-review-initiative)

- **FAIR Data Evaluation Framework Receives Community Endorsement**  
  A new framework for assessing FAIR data has been quickly endorsed by institutions, aiming to standardize measurement of data openness and reusability.  
  [Source](https://www.go-fair.org/news/fair-data-evaluation-framework)

- **Research Data Alliance Highlights Skills Gap in Open Science Training**  
  The Research Data Alliance has identified a critical need for enhanced training in open data stewardship and research software practices.  
  [Source](https://researchdataalliance.org/news/open-science-training-focus)

- **Academic Libraries Expand Open Science Support Services**  
  Libraries are enhancing their services to support open access publishing, data management, and compliance with Open Science mandates.  
  [Source](https://sparcopen.org/news/library-led-open-science-services)
